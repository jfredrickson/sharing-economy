Sharing Economy Conference: Boston

This conference introduces the idea that ownership and access are shared between corporations, start-ups, non-governmental organizations, and people. The Sharing Economy results in new products, services, and businesses that can combine resources to enable more access, knowledge, tools, and in the end, more productivity.

According to a recent report from Altimeter Group, an independent research firm, the sharing economy is a $2 billion industry already with a project value of over $100 billion.

The sharing economy includes a range of structures including for-profit, non-profit, barter and co-operative. The sharing economy enables expanded access to products, services and talent beyond one owner. Corporations, governments and individuals all actively participate as buyers, sellers, lenders or borrowers in these varied and evolving organizational structures. Shared offerings are based on a set of values that includes trust, transparency, economic empowerment, creative expression, authenticity, community resilience and human connection.

Join experts in business, law, policy, sociology, and environmental planning to discuss how the Sharing Economy can benefit the Boston community and beyond.

Denise comes from the future of journalism world with extensive experience in participatory media and community building.   • Juliet Schor

    Boston College: Sociology Scholar

    Show Info

    Juliet Schor is Professor of Sociology at Boston College. Before joining Boston College, she taught at Harvard University for 17 years, in the Department of Economics and the Committee on Degrees in Women's Studies. A graduate of Wesleyan University, Schor received her Ph.D. in economics at the University of Massachusetts. Schor is currently working on issues of environmental sustainability and their relation to Americans' lifestyles and the economy and the emergence of a conscious consumption movement.   • Julian Agyeman

    Tufts University

    Show Info

    Julian Agyeman is a Professor of Urban and Environmental Policy and Planning at Tufts University, Medford, MA. He is the originator of the concept of 'just sustainability', the full integration of social justice and sustainability, defined as 'the need to ensure a better quality of life for all, now and into the future, in a just and equitable manner, whilst living within the limits of supporting ecosystems.'

    He is co-founder, and Editor-in-Chief of the international journal 'Local Environment: The International Journal of Justice and Sustainability.' With over 150 publications, his books include 'Just Sustainabilities: Development in an Unequal World' (MIT Press 2003); 'Sustainable Communities and the Challenge of Environmental Justice' (NYU Press 2005); 'Environmental Inequalities Beyond Borders: Local Perspectives on Global Injustices' (MIT Press 2011); 'Cultivating Food Justice: Race, Class and Sustainability' (MIT Press 2011); and 'Introducing Just Sustainabilities: Policy, Planning and Practice' (Zed Books 2013).
